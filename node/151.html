Indiana buys 150 miles for rail trails
(Feb. 2008) - With a goal to have every Hoosier living within 15 minutes of a recreational trail within the next 10 years, Indiana has agreed to purchase 150 miles of unused railway corridors. According to Gov. Mitch Daniels, the purchase is part of a “quantum leap forward” for the state. The 400-parcel purchase agreement will cost $1.5 million and include parcels from 39 counties. The state’s rail-trail system is already popular among commuters and includes well-liked recreational pathways like the Monon Trail in Indianapolis. Once the purchase is complete, the state of Indiana will turn the land over to local governments, businesses and nonprofit organizations for development. Terms of the deal with the former Penn Central Rail Line won’t be finalized until December.

Adventure Cycling launches new day trip maps with Ohio destination
(Feb. 2008) - Freedom’s Landing in Ripley, Ohio, is the first in Adventure Cycling’s planned launch of a series of free-to-download maps for cyclists. The Freedom’s Landing map details three loops along the Underground Railroad Bicycle Route with distances of 9, 13 and 27 miles, and skill levels ranging from beginner to advanced cyclist. The loops are color-coded and show terrain changes, nearby restaurants and historic buildings along the route. Adventure Cycling encourages all riders to create their own maps along the Underground Railroad route that can be posted and promoted on its website. For more information on the Underground Railroad Bicycle Route, check out our feature story.

Get Out! launches
It’s official - Get Out! has officially gotten out, er, gone or come out, uh … we’ve officially launched! GO! is your monthly adventure travel webzine dedicated to covering (and uncovering) outdoor gems you’ll love within the Ohio River region. We aim to accommodate all levels of outdoor enthusiasts, from beginners to hard-core adventurers. Whatever your skills, whether your passion is hiking, cycling, paddling, snow sports or any other active outdoor pursuit, GO! covers areas you’ll want to visit.

“Get Out! provides something that was previously missing - a clearinghouse of thorough, reliable information on outdoor destinations close to home,” said Mary Reed, Get Out! editor. “The beauty of defining ourselves by a natural boundary, the Ohio River watershed, is that we are connected by place but not separated by artificial political boundaries.” GO! covers western PA, OH, WV, KY, IN and portions of MD and IL.
